Old is inclined, flg. BR, to emend voc. suhasti yaḥ to *suhasti yāḥ, derived from the stem suhásti ya-, hence a masc. nom. pl. referring to the priestly officiants. Re follows him, remarking rather scornfully “il est peu probable que l’auteur ait voulu désigner par ce Voc. … des entités féminines, à la rigueur toutefois les « doigts ».” But see Ge’s quite sensible n.

suggesting that the fem. refers to the fingers, which are frequently assigned ritual agency in the IXth Maṇḍala. Note that the ten fingers (dáśa kṣípaḥ) explicitly occur two vss. later (6b).

śukrā́… manthínā is a discontinuous dual dvandva. The phraseology matches III.32.2 gávāśiram manthínam ... śukrám ... sómam; cf. góbhiḥ śrīṇīta in c.
