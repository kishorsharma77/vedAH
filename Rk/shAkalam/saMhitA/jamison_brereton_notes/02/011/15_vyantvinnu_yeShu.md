And another -- or rather the same mandasānáḥ in the nominal rel. clause introduced by yéṣu.

JPB supplies “be” as the verb in pāda c; I’m inclined to follow Ge (/WG) in supplying a form of √vṛdh ‘strengthen’, generated from ávardhayaḥ beginning d. Ge suggests the impv. vardhaya. It would also be possible simply to read ávardhayaḥ with both pādas: “you have strengthened us in battles (and) have strengthened heaven …”]
